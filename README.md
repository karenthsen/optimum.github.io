# Measurement of fetal brainwaves in the third trimester of pregnancy

## Description:
We’re recruiting for an exciting study that will take place in the third trimester of pregnancy.
Our research explores how your baby’s brain begins to respond to light and sound before birth! We’ll use sensors on your abdomen to measure fetal brainwaves. You will also receive an ultrasound image.

The study will take place at Aarhus University Hospital in Skejby. You are welcome to bring a person of your choice to accompany you to both meetings and the laboratory session. Join us and help unlock the secrets of early brain development!

If you are interested in this study, please contact us at: optimum@cfin.au.dk

Project leaders: Prof. Sarang Dalal and Prof. Lars Henning Pedersen

## Eligibility Requirements:
You must be at least 32 weeks pregnant at the time of the scan. You must be healthy with no pregnancy complications or special risk factors.

## Projekttitel: Fosterets hjernereaktioner på lys og lyd målt med optisk pumpede magnetometre
Du er inviteret til at deltage i en forskningsundersøgelse. Før du beslutter dig, er det vigtigt, at du forstår, hvorfor forskningen bliver lavet, og hvad den indebærer. Tag dig god tid til at læse informationen grundigt og diskuter det gerne med andre. Spørg os endelig, hvis der er noget, der er uklart, eller om du ønsker mere information. ### Hvad er formålet med denne undersøgelse?
Undersøgelsen har til formål at måle hjerte- og hjerneaktiviteten hos fostre ved hjælp af en ny magnetisk sensorteknologi. Vi ønsker primært at måle hvordan fosterets hjerne reagerer på lyde og lys.
Vi håber, at disse målinger vil bane vejen for, at sensorerne kan bruges til mere detaljeret at undersøge hvordan den menneskelige hjerne udvikler sig. Når teknikken er etableret, vil den forhåbentligt også kunne bruges til at overvåge fosteret i komplicerede graviditeter, f.eks. ved svær væksthæmning. 

### Er der nogen nytte ved at deltage i studiet?
Du vil modtage en fosterultralydsscanning og, hvis ønskeligt, et print af scanningen. Deltagelse i undersøgelsen vil ikke påvirke din normale prænatale eller medicinske behandling.
 
Du modtager 150 DKK/time for deltagelse i studiet. Du vil derfor få 300 DKK for hver typisk 2-timers session. Denne kompensation er skattepligtig som B-indkomst for indbyggere i Danmark. 

### Hvem kan deltage?
Du kan deltage i dette studie, hvis:
• du er gravid og 18 år eller ældre
• du er ved god helbredstilstand
• din graviditet ikke har haft komplikationer eller særlige risikofaktorer
• du er tilgængelig i de specifikke graviditetsuger, der er relevante for studiet (alle muligheder efter uge 25)

### Hvad vil der ske med mig, og hvad forventes der, hvis jeg deltager?
Eksperimentet består enten af en enkelt session eller 3 sessioner med cirka 4 ugers mellemrum, med en maksimal varighed på 2 timer pr. session. Før vi begynder, vil vi opsummere, hvordan eksperimentet vil forløbe, og du kan stille alle de spørgsmål, du måtte have. Du er velkommen til at medbringe én person efter eget valg til laboratoriesessionen.

Du vil blive bedt om at skifte til hospitalstøj efter ankomsten. Du vil blive bedt om at fjerne genstande, der kan indeholde metal, for eksempel en bøjle-bh og smykker (ringe, øreringe, piercinger osv.), så du foretrækker måske at lade dem blive hjemme.

Først vil du modtage en ultralydsscanning for at bestemme fosterets position i livmoderen og måle fosterets hjerterytme. Denne scanning er den samme som rutinemæssige ultralydsscanninger for f.eks. fosterets vækst eller hvis der mistanke om underkropspræsentation. 

Herefter vil du blive hjulpet ind i MEG (magnetoencefalografi) lokalet. Du vil blive bedt om at lægge dig på sengen eller sætte dig i stolen inde i kabinen. Hvis eksperimentet vil bruge lysblink, vil du blive bedt om at eksponere din mave under optagelsen, så lyset kan nå din hud og dit foster. 

Såkaldte optisk pumpede magnetometre, små magnetiske sensorer, vil blive placeret på din mave for at måle dit barns hjerte- og hjerneaktivitet. Sensorerne kan også registrere din egen hjerteaktivitet. Sensorerne skal føles behageligt varme. Vi vil bede dig om at undlade bevægelser så meget som muligt under optagelsen, så vi vil arbejde sammen med dig for at sikre den mest behagelige position, f.eks. med ekstra puder eller et tæppe.

Vi kan tage billeder for at dokumentere sensorernes position omkring din krop.

Lyset vil derefter blive dæmpet, og vi lukker døren. Kabinen er lydisoleret, men vi kan høre dig via mikrofon og kommunikere med dig via et intercom system I dette eksperiment vil du blive bedt om at slappe af og blive i samme position så vidt muligt – du må gerne sove, hvis du vil!

Vi vil forsøge at måle din babys hjerneaktivitet som reaktion på lysglimt og/eller korte lyde. Lysblinkene og lydene overstiger ikke intensiteten af daglig eksponering. Du kan få udleveret høretelefoner, der spiller lavniveau hvid støj eller din ønskede playliste. Vi vil også foretage målinger under hvile uden ekstern stimulation.

MEG-optagelsen vil tage maksimalt 100 minutter, men typisk 70-80 minutter i alt.

### Hvilke enheder er involveret?
Vi vil placere optisk pumpede magnetometre, som er ultrafølsomme magnetiske sensorer, på din mave for at måle dit barns hjerte- og hjerneaktivitet samt din egen hjerteaktivitet. 

### Hvad er de mulige risici eller bivirkninger ved at deltage?
Der er ingen sikkerhedsrisici forbundet med eksperimentet eller sensorerne. Sensorerne arbejder ved lave spændinger, er fuldstændig forseglet i plastik og bruger ikke stråling, kemikalier eller stærke magneter.  Nogle deltagere kan finde MEG-kabinen klaustrofobisk, men husk, at du kan kommunikere med os og bede om at tage en pause eller stoppe helt, hvis du bliver utilpas.

De magnetiske målinger er udelukkende til videnskabelige formål og er ikke optimeret til at opdage kliniske abnormiteter. Normalt vil dataene ikke blive gennemgået af kvalificeret medicinsk personale. Ikke desto mindre er der en fjern mulighed for, at de magnetiske målinger eller ultralyd kan antyde en hidtil ukendt medicinsk bekymring. I dette tilfælde vil vi konsultere en læge, som vil informere dig, hvis yderligere undersøgelser eller en konsultation anbefales.

## Title of Project: Fetal brain responses to light and sound measured with optically pumped magnetometers
You are invited to take part in a research study. Before you decide, it is important for you to understand why the research is being done and what it involves. Please take time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with others, if you wish. Ask us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. We can present information about the study in person or by video call, if desired. Take time to decide whether or not you wish to take part. Thank you for reading this and considering our study.

### What is the purpose of this study?
This study aims to measure the heart and brain activity of the developing human fetus, using a new magnetic sensor technology. The main goal will be to measure the fetal brain responses to sound and light.
We hope these measurements will serve as a proof-of-concept for using these sensors in future experiments to study how the human brain develops in greater detail. Once the technique is established, it may also prove useful for monitoring fetal brain development in high-risk pregnancies.

### Are there any benefits to participating in the study?
You will receive a fetal ultrasound scan and, if desired, a print of the scan. Participation in the study will not affect your normal prenatal or medical care.

You will receive 150 DKK/hour for participation in the study. You will therefore be compensated 300 DKK for each typical session of 2 hours. This compensation is taxable as B-income for residents of Denmark.

### Who can take part?
You can take part in this study if:
• you are pregnant and 18 years of age or older
• you are in good health
• your pregnancy has not had complications or special risk factors
• you are available during the specific weeks of pregnancy relevant to the study (all possibilities after week 25)

### What will happen to me and what is expected if I take part?
The experiment consists of either a single session, or 3 sessions approximately 4 weeks apart, with a maximum duration of 2 hours per session. Before we begin, we will summarize how the experiment will go, and you can ask any remaining questions you might have. You are welcome to bring a person of your choice to accompany you to the laboratory session.

You will be asked to change into hospital clothing after arrival. You will be asked to remove items that may contain metal, for example an underwire bra and any jewelry (rings, earrings, piercings, etc.), so you may prefer to leave these at home.

First, you will receive an ultrasound scan to determine the position of the fetus within the womb and estimate the fetal heart rate. This scan will be very similar to the routine ultrasound scan you will have already done as part of your prenatal care.

After this, you will be helped into the MEG (magnetoencephalography) cabin. You will be asked to lay down on the bed or sit down in the chair inside the cabin. If the experiment will use light flashes, then you will be asked to expose your abdomen for the duration of the recording, so that the light can reach your skin and your baby inside.

Optically pumped magnetometers, small magnetic sensors, will be placed on your abdomen to measure your baby’s heart and brain activity. The sensors can also capture your own heart activity. The sensors should feel pleasantly warm. We will ask you to refrain from movements as much as possible during the recording, so we will work with you to ensure the most comfortable position. We can provide extra pillows or a blanket, for example.

We may take photos to document the position of the sensors around your body.

The lights will then be dimmed and we will close the door. The cabin is sound insulated, but we will be able to hear you through a microphone and communicate with you through the intercom. For this experiment, you will be asked to relax and remain in the same position to the extent possible – you may sleep if you like!

We will attempt to measure your baby’s brain activity in response to light flashes and/or brief sounds. The light flashes and sounds do not exceed the intensity of everyday exposure. You may be provided with earphones playing low-level white noise or your desired playlist. We will also make measurements during rest with no external stimulation.

The MEG recording will take a maximum of 100 minutes, but typically 70-80 minutes in total.

### What are the devices involved?
We will place optically pumped magnetometers, which are ultra-sensitive magnetic sensors, on your abdomen to measure your baby’s heart and brain activity, as well as your own heart activity. 

### What are the possible risks or side effects of taking part?
There are no safety risks posed by the experiment or the sensors. The sensors operate at low voltages, are completely sealed in plastic, and do not use radiation, chemicals, or strong magnets.  Some participants may find the MEG cabin claustrophobic, but please remember that you can communicate with us and ask to take a break or stop completely if you become uncomfortable.

The magnetic measurements are purely for scientific purposes and are not optimized to detect clinical abnormalities. Ordinarily the data will not be reviewed by qualified medical personnel. Nevertheless, there is a remote possibility that the magnetic measurements or ultrasound may suggest a previously unknown medical concern. In this case, we will consult with an appropriate clinical staff member, who will inform you if further medical consultation is recommended.

### Confidentiality - who will have access to the data?
Your participation is confidential and will not be included in your medical record. No personally identifying information (such as your name or CPR number) is ever stored with the data, though basic demographic information such as your age and week of pregnancy will be. Fully anonymized data may be made available in public databases, with your consent. Any photographs will not be included in such public databases. Processed results may be published or presented as discussed in the next section.

### What happens at the end of the study?
Fully anonymized data and the study results may be published in a journal, included in grant applications, or used for teaching purposes. With your consent, they may also be included in public databases. The scientific results may also be presented at scientific meetings or in talks at academic institutions. Results will always be presented in a way that ensures that individual participants cannot be identified.

You may ask more questions about the study at any time - before, during and after the study. Please do not hesitate to ask the experimenter if there are any points about the study that remain unclear to you. ### Can I withdraw from the study?
Your participation in this research project is voluntary, and you may withdraw from the research at any time and for any reason, without explaining why, and this will not affect your legal rights.

### Can the investigators interrupt the study?
The study may likewise be interrupted by the investigators at any time, for any variety of reasons, for example technical difficulties or a change in the eligibility criteria.
